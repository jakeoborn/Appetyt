## Peter Luger Steak House (Steakhouse, Williamsburg)
Score: 99 · Price: $4
Awards: Michelin Recommended, James Beard America's Classics

Cash only, no reservations for parties under 4, waiters who've been there longer than you've been alive, and the greatest porterhouse in the history of steak. Peter Luger has been slinging dry-aged beef in Williamsburg since 1887 and couldn't care less about your Yelp review. The German fried potatoes and creamed spinach are non-negotiable sides. Get the tomato and onion salad — it has no right being that good at a steakhouse.

Signature dishes: Porterhouse for Two, German Fried Potatoes, Thick-Cut Bacon

## Carbone (Italian-American, Greenwich Village)
Score: 99 · Price: $4
Awards: Michelin 1-Star

The most theatrical Italian restaurant in America. The tuxedoed waiters, the Sinatra soundtrack, the spicy rigatoni vodka that launched a thousand Instagram reels — Carbone is dinner as performance art. Major Food Group turned a Little Italy red-sauce joint into a $400 experience, and somehow it's worth it. Getting a reservation is harder than getting into Harvard. The veal parm is the size of your head.

Signature dishes: Spicy Rigatoni Vodka, Veal Parmesan, Caesar Salad

## COTE Korean Steakhouse (Korean BBQ / Steakhouse, Flatiron / NoMad)
Score: 94 · Price: $4
Awards: Michelin 1-Star, James Beard Best New Restaurant 2018

The world's first Michelin-starred Korean BBQ, and it earns that star on every visit. Simon Kim created a Korean steakhouse where USDA Prime galbi meets a $1,000+ wine list, and somehow it all makes sense. The butcher's feast is the move — let the kitchen decide. The omakase-style counter seats are the best in the house. Flatiron location, impeccable service, and a buzz that never dies.

Signature dishes: Butcher's Feast, USDA Prime Galbi, Egg Souffle, Steak Omakase

## Sadelle's (Jewish Deli & Brunch, SoHo)
Score: 88 · Price: $3

Sadelle's is what happens when Major Food Group opens a Jewish deli. The tower of smoked fish on a platter is absurdly photogenic and genuinely excellent. The bagels are made in-house, the sticky buns are legendary, and the dining room feels like a Hollywood set. Expensive for a deli? Absolutely. Worth it? Also absolutely. SoHo brunch at its most theatrical.

Signature dishes: Bagel Tower, Smoked Fish Platter, Sticky Buns

---

## Nobu Downtown (Japanese, Financial District)
Score: 88 · Price: $3

Nobu Matsuhisa's Financial District outpost serves the Japanese-Peruvian fusion that launched a global empire. The black cod miso is the dish that made Nobu famous — buttery, sweet, and caramelized to perfection. The yellowtail jalapeno and the rock shrimp tempura are equally essential. The space is massive and buzzy. It's not cheap, it's not quiet, and it's still one of the best splurges downtown.

Signature dishes: Black Cod Miso, Rock Shrimp Tempura, Yellowtail Jalapeño

---

## Scarr's Pizza (Pizza, Lower East Side)
Score: 90 · Price: $2
Awards: NYT Critics Pick

Scarr Pimentel mills his own organic flour in the basement, which makes his plain slice one of the most talked-about in New York. The shop on Orchard feels like a time warp — wood paneling, old booths, and a crowd that runs from LES locals to visiting chefs. The pepperoni slice with a glossy curl of grease is the move. Cash-friendly, late-night friendly, and genuinely better than the hype.

Signature dishes: Plain Slice, Pepperoni Slice, Sicilian Square

---

## Sweetgreen (Salads / Fast Casual, Midtown)
Score: 80 · Price: $2

Sweetgreen turned salad into a tech-enabled lifestyle brand that somehow works. The Harvest Bowl and Crispy Rice Bowl are reliably good, the ingredients are genuinely fresh, and the app ordering is annoyingly efficient. Multiple Manhattan locations. Not exciting food, but honest food, and sometimes a $16 salad on a Tuesday is exactly what adulting requires.

Signature dishes: Harvest Bowl, Kale Caesar, Seasonal Bowl
